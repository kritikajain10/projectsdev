JEE 2025 Exam Eligibility Criteria

The eligibility criteria for JEE Main 2025 and JEE Advanced 2025 are outlined below. Candidates must meet specific requirements related to education, age, and other eligibility criteria for JEE Main 2025 and JEE Advanced 2025.

JEE Main 2025 Exam Eligibility Criteria

Candidates must meet specific requirements to appear for JEE Main 2025:

Criteria Details
Educational Qualification Must have completed Class 12 or an equivalent examination conducted by a recognized board/institution.
Year of Passing Candidates who passed Class 12 in 2023, are appearing in 2024, or took a drop year in 2024 can apply.
Subjects Required Must have Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics/Biology/Technical Vocational Subject. For B.Arch: Mathematics is mandatory.
Percentage of Marks General category: Minimum 75% marks or top 20 percentile in the respective board.
SC/ST category: Minimum 65% marks.
Age Limit No upper age limit for JEE Main.
Number of Attempts No limit on attempts after completing Class 12.

JEE Advanced 2025 Exam Eligibility Criteria

Candidates must meet specific requirements to appear for JEE Advanced 2025:

Criteria Details
Performance in JEE Main Must be among the top 2,50,000 successful candidates in the B.E./B.Tech (Paper 1) of JEE Main 2025.
Category-Wise Distribution GEN-EWS: 10%, OBC-NCL: 27%, SC: 15%, ST: 7.5%, OPEN: 40.5%.
5% horizontal reservation for PwD across all categories.
Age Limit Born on or after October 1, 2000.
SC/ST/PwD: Born on or after October 1, 1995 (5-year relaxation).
Number of Attempts Maximum of two attempts in two consecutive years.

JEE 2025 Preparation Tips for Main and Advanced

Preparing for JEE 2025 requires a disciplined and structured approach. Here are some preparation tips for JEE 2025 exam that you can use to strengthen your exam preparation:

  • Understand the Exam Pattern and Syllabus: Familiarize yourself with the structure, marking scheme, and important topics.
  • Create a Study Schedule: Develop a realistic timetable that covers all subjects and allows time for revision.
  • Focus on Conceptual Clarity: Strengthen fundamental concepts across Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics.
  • Use NCERT Books: Start with NCERT for a strong foundation before moving to advanced reference books.
  • Practice Daily: Regular problem-solving is essential to develop speed and accuracy.
  • Take Mock Tests: Simulate exam conditions to build confidence and improve time management.
  • Analyze Weak Areas: Regularly review performance in tests and focus on improving weaker sections.
  • Revise Consistently: Keep revisiting important formulas, reactions, and concepts.
  • Solve Previous Year Papers: Practice with previous papers to understand question trends and difficulty levels.
  • Seek Guidance When Needed: Don’t hesitate to ask teachers or peers for help with challenging topics.
